solutions

With these problem areas established, my team and I then conducted further research to better understand the domain and gather supporting information.



Once completed, we consolidated our findings and created various solution-based brainstorming. We each tried to come up with bizzare or almost fictitious designs that pushed ourselves to display the sum of all of our work.

We proceeded to compare and determine which of the solutions to move on with by using an Importance/Difficulty Matrix , and eventually settled on the mobile app. Although we weren't surprised that we settled on such a direction, we were able to incorporate insights we gained from ideating a variety of different solutions.

reflection

This hackathon was a perfect opportunity for me to cap off my University career. Although I was excited to be a part of it, I was honestly largely nervous and anxious; this was amplified throughout the project as it was a very different experience being thrust into a project and being let loose versus being walked through a course project week by week (even though in class it felt like we were being fully autonomous).

As a whole, I am extremely proud of the work my team and I was able to produce. This is especially because our interface was chosen by one judge by the best in the client group, and also was selected by our client to be used as reference for their official mobile app.
